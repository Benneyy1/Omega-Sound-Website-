Founder & CEO

Benjamin "Benneyy" Schultz

Benjamin's understanding of music was shaped far from any concert stage. Trained in sacred Kete drumming and dance under Emmanuel Attah Poku at the Kumasi Cultural Centre in Ghana, in the Vodun traditions of Benin under Orphée Géograph, and in the streets of San Juan, Puerto Rico performing alongside Plenazo Tribe he came to know music not as a career path but as a tool for emotional release, communal bonding, and self-expression and healing.

Then he came back to the West.

A graduate of the California Institute of the Arts with a double specialization in Music Technology and Jazz Drums, Benjamin went on to perform at the world's largest stage: Super Bowl LV, alongside Bad Bunny, in front of over 120 million people. He has also performed with Lauren Jauregui and across genres bridging Latin, pop, experimental, r&b, hip hop, jazz and West African traditions.

What set those worlds apart told him everything. In the sacred circles of West Africa and in the comparsas of Festival La Calle San Sebastián, music belonged to everyone every body moving, every voice part of the sound. Then came the Super Bowl: 120 million people watching one man perform what they were born to create themselves.

That gap is the reason Omega Sound Inc. exists.

Music's deepest power to release tension, foster connection, and heal only activates in the act of making it. Not streaming it. Not watching it. Creating it. Yet the Western world has built an entire culture around consuming music produced by a tiny fraction of the population, while the rest are convinced they're "not musical."

Benjamin founded Omega Sound Inc. to dismantle that belief with technology. Omega is the world's first handheld instrument that translates natural body movement into real-time music. No notes to learn. No technique to master. Just your body, your gesture, and the sound that proves what Benjamin has always believed: "Everyone is already creative. I just need to prove it to them."

His mission: a world where creativity is not a talent reserved for the few, but a daily practice embraced by all, transforming how people live, connect, and heal.

"Life is a performance. Creation makes it worth living."
